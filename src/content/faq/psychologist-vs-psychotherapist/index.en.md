---
question: "What is the difference between a psychologist and a psychopraticien?"
---
In France, the title of psychologue (psychologist) has been protected by law since 1985 and requires a master's degree (Bac+5) in psychology from a French university. The title of psychothérapeute (psychotherapist) has also been regulated since 2010 and requires registration on the national register (RPPS).

A psychopraticien (psychotherapy practitioner) is a professional trained in psychotherapy whose title is freely used and not regulated by the French state. They work with individuals, couples, families, and groups. Professional federations such as the FF2P set rigorous standards: in-depth training in clinical psychopathology, personal psychotherapy, ongoing supervision, and adherence to a code of ethics.

Aimee Cote holds a master's degree in Marriage and Family Therapy from the University of New Hampshire (UNH), a COAMFTE-accredited programme that included 500 hours of supervised clinical practice. She practises as a psychopraticienne specialising in systemic couple and family therapy, and conducts sessions in French, English, and Spanish.
